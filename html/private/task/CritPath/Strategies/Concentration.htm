Strategy:  Develop a set of criteria and use those criteria to recommend new concentrations to add and existing concentrations to no longer offer.

 

 

Action Step / Result

Date

Responsibility

 

 

Key distinctive competency phrases:  Student Centered and Add Immediate Value.

 

 

Positioning Statement

 

The task force has concluded that a key distinguishing feature of the OCOB is that it prepares

students to hit the ground running while also providing them with the sophistication to become

leaders of organizations.  The task force believes that a primary role of concentrations is to provide sufficient depth in a course of study to enable OCOB students to provide immediate value to

business organizations.  This is not to say that concentrations should be viewed as more important

than other disciplines, for a successful businessperson must have a breadth of understanding in numerous important areas.  Rather, concentrations are merely a logical way to organize academic programs to make the college distinctive.

 

 

•   Should consider the following recommendations from the Strategic Planning Team:

 

     –   Maintain concentrations in Accounting, Finance, Human Resources, Management Information Systems, and Marketing.

 

     –   Phase out concentrations in Business and Industrial Economics, International Trade and Development, International Management, and Management.

 

     –   Consider adding a concentration in Small Business Management.
