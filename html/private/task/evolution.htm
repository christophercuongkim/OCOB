Evolution of Purpose aka Mission Statement

 

May 3:  Five initial drafts from five teams

 

Ø      Through a student oriented learn by doing educational approach the OCOB prepares highly motivated students to live and work responsibly in a changing technological environment

 

Ø      The OCOB is a learn-by-doing, innovation polytechnic learning community where faculty work closely with students to advance knowledge in innovative programs directed towards placement of students in industry.

 

Ø      Building on our polytechnic tradition, the OCOB bridges the gap between theory and practice, educating leaders of tomorrow through innovative curriculum, inspiring life-long learning social responsibility and positive contributions to society.

 

o       We value:  diversity, partnerships with industry, enhanced teaching through applied research.

o       We demand:  excellence in faculty, staff and students, collegiality and a supportive community

 

Ø      The OCOB prepares exception students who have a distinctive combination of practical  business knowledge, technical skills, social responsibility

 

Ø      Within a polytechnic environment, the OCOB prepares California’s top students for successful lives and careers in a rapidly changing world.

May 4: Comments raised during discussion

 

  • Add the word “technology” in statement.
  • The statement does not reference our role in knowledge creation -  intellectual leadership.
  • How does bridging the gap lead to educating tomorrow’s business leaders
  • Mission should make a strong statement about student-centeredness” to “educating.”
  • Contributions to both theory and practice, now just riding the gap.
  • Should we identify the “through” (i.e. how-to) elements.
  • Should reference our contributions to both theory and practice, not just bridging the gap.
  • No need to reference “technology.”

            Everyone can make that statement.

 

  • Be careful not to over-emphasize the student in directing his/her own education program.
  • “Learn by doing” is an important distinction of Cal Poly.
  • “Preparing” not “educating.”
  • Our “niche” is being a student-oriented, teaching institution.  (We attract top quality students.)
  • Broaden the notion of innovation beyond “programs” to include, for example, faculty, learning, etc.
  • All schools are, should be, preparing students for a changing world.
  • IS the mission a statement of what we are, what we want to be (or what we are becoming)?
  • How can the mission answer the questions of who, what, where, when, how?
  • Mission should reference our particular strengths, niche – not necessarily the “how to.”
  • Should we make any reference to our geographic service area?
  • Student-centeredness is a special quality of OCOB that should be referenced in the Mission Statement.
  • Mission should provide some clarity about the relative emphasis on research/scholarship and teaching.

  • The statement does not distinguish OCOB as a teaching, student-oriented place (vs. a research I institution)
  • Need much greater clarity on the relative balance of teaching and research.
