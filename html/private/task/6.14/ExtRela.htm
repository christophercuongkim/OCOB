Distinctive Capabilities

 

Strategies

The COB is the recognized leader among institutions of higher learning in California for providing a "hands-on", technology-oriented undergraduate education that is highly valued by employers.

 

1.    Develop informative and impactful communication with stakeholders to foster mutually beneficial relationships with alumni, parents, recruiters and industry.

 

 

      

 

 

 

 

 

 

Measures

 

2.    Partner with industry and potential industry employers to develop targeted and high value-added curricula and educational opportunities.

1.      Hiring rates of undergraduates are in the top 10% of all colleges and universities in California in terms of starting salary range, and they are being accepted in targeted industries among leading companies.

2.      On satisfaction surveys, constituents give the COB high assessments (at least an 8 on a 10-point scale) for the following attributes:

a.      Recruiters for the “hireability” of COB graduates

b.      Parents for the educational quality of COB.

c.      Graduating students, one year after graduation, for the value of their education.

3.      The financial contributions of parents and companies grow 10% annually.

a.      Amount of monetary and in-kind contributions by alumni

b.      Amount of monetary and in-kind contributions by non-alumni

4.      Dollar amount of scholarships

5.      Number of students hired or rate of placement through career services

6.      Starting salary of graduates

7.      Number of internships

8.      Quality and number of employers

9.      Number of joint projects/programs with industry

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

3.    Develop activities and programs to make college recruiters/employers’ experience with COB extremely positive and productive.

 

 

 

 

 

 

4.    Proactively influence and monitor the measures that determine the U.S. News and World Report ranking of college business programs.

 

 

 

 

 

 

5.    Communicate and partner with the other colleges: for example, Engineering in using the planned Technology Research Park to build valuable co-op opportunities for students, teaching and applied research opportunities for faculty and incubator opportunities for companies.
