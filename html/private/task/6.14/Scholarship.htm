Distinctive Capabilities

 

Strategies

 

 

 

Faculty engaged in a diverse mix of discovery, integration, articulation and application of knowledge in a polytechnic, student-centered culture that fosters learning and adds value to organizations (business, government, and community).

 

A)  Develop a plan for hiring outstanding teachers with strong training and interest in scholarship related to their teaching.

B)  Secure/improve resources – including funds, assigned time for professional development and scholarship.

C)  Have workload/schedule flexibility that enhances the production of scholarship while maintaining emphasis on teaching.

D)  Develop exchanges with industry – fellowships, internships, visiting scholars, executive-in-residence, etc.

E)  Create Research forums (college-wide, faculty and students).

F)    Review and change senior projects to focus on scholarship/research.

G)   Review, and where necessary, revise RPT and FAR documents.

H)  Provide incentives to promote scholarship appropriate to OCOB mission:

1.       Seeking and securing funding for instructionally-related research--corporate, government, and private foundation grants, gifts, or contracts.

2.       Make an explicit statement that all four Scholarships (teaching, Discovery, Integration and Application) are valued.

3.       Recognize the (potential) value of faculty internships in industry or with government or independent-sector organizations.
